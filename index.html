Symbols

Whips
In Kindred, whips symbolize the white man’s capacity for violence and oppression. In the novel, the men who wield whips are to be feared; they use violence to get what they want. The whips symbolize the evil of racism and slavery.

Birthdays
In Kindred, birthdays symbolize the cruel cycle of slavery. For the slaves on the plantation, though the birth of a child brings great joy, it also brings the suffering of being more reliant on staying at the plantation. It also brings the fear that the child will become a slave and may be sold away.

Maps
In Kindred, maps symbolize the possibility of freedom. Because of this, it is extremely dangerous to own a map. A person with a map can find their way, while a person without one can only go as far as their own personal experience and memory allows.

Theme

The Corrupting Influence of Power
Rufus, a child burdened with societal expectations, embodies the corrupting influence of power. While initially driven by compassion and respect for his friends Alice and Nigel, despite their different backgrounds, the inheritance thrusts him into a position of dominance. This newfound authority unleashes a latent arrogance within him, transforming him into a "small tyrant" who abuses his friends and elders. His transformation mirrors that of his father, Tom Weylin, who descends from a "routinely brutal" master into a capricious despot, driven by the insatiable appetite for more control.

Literary Elements

Foreshadowing
Excerpt: Dana's first time traveling triggers a feeling of impending danger: "There was a sense of foreboding, even before I saw the smoke. It hung as a premonition in the air so thick it seemed I could taste it."

Meaning: This foreshadows the violence and brutality Dana will encounter in the antebellum South. It establishes a sense of unease and suspense, propelling the reader forward.

Hero's Journey: This aligns with the "Call to Adventure" stage, where the hero encounters a challenge that disrupts their ordinary life.

Diction and Syntax
Excerpt: "He stood, staring at me, his jaw slack, eyes glazed. Then, as though a string had been cut, he crumpled, whimpering. He cradled his bloody head in his arms and rocked slowly back and forth."

Meaning: The fragmented syntax and vivid imagery evoke the shock and pain of witnessing brutality. It creates a sense of immediacy and emotional impact for the reader.

Hero's Journey: This echoes the "Approach" stage, where the hero must confront their deepest fears and prepare for the final ordeal. The diction and syntax heighten the tension and underscore the stakes of Dana's journey.
